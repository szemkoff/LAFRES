## Major line of work (aligned across lab and planning docs)

The program focuses on **quantitative understanding and optimization** of **acoustic amplification of solid-state D–D fusion** in titanium deuteride systems—not plasma confinement at tokamak scale.

1. **CFD and bubble dynamics** — OpenFOAM-based modeling of D₂O bubble collapse and validation against experiment.  
2. **Microfluidic control** — Monodisperse bubbles and controlled emulsions (e.g., Elveflow-class hardware, custom chips).  
3. **Detection and data** — Multi-counter neutron detection, timing, and statistical confidence for small rate changes.  
4. **High-speed diagnostics** — Imaging and correlation with acoustic drive and neutron signatures.  
5. **Scaling and roadmap** — Documented paths from demonstrated performance toward application-relevant neutron yields and future phases.

This sequence matches the **technical and management** narrative used in internal program planning and federal research submissions.
