# Literature Review

## 1. Inertial Electrostatic Confinement Fusion

### Key References

1. **Farnsworth (1966)**: "Method and apparatus for producing nuclear-fusion reactions" - Original IEC patent
2. **Miley & Murali (2014)**: *Inertial Electrostatic Confinement (IEC) Fusion* - Comprehensive textbook
3. **Thorson et al. (2011)**: "Neutron production from IEC device" - Experimental neutron yields

### Key Findings
- IEC devices routinely achieve 10⁶-10⁹ n/s with modest power (&lt;10 kW)
- Efficiency typically 10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁴ (far from break-even)
- Suitable for neutron source applications, not power generation

## 2. Liquid Lithium Blankets

### Key References

1. **ORNL/TM-4229 (1973)**: "Compatibility of Lithium with Structural Materials"
2. **Smolentsev et al. (2015)**: "Liquid metal flow in fusion blankets" - MHD effects
3. **Jauch et al. (2018)**: "Thermophysical Properties of Lithium"

### Key Findings
- Molybdenum and tungsten show excellent lithium compatibility at 400-500°C
- Natural convection in lithium: Ra~10⁷ (turbulent regime expected)
- Corrosion manageable with proper material selection and temperature control

## 3. Acoustic Control of Fluids

### Key References

1. **Lee & Wang (1990)**: "Acoustic radiation force and streaming"
2. **Dual & Möller (2012)**: "Acoustic levitation in microgravity"
3. **Leighton (1994)**: *The Acoustic Bubble*

### Key Findings
- Radiation pressure: P_rad = &lt;p²&gt;/(ρc²)
- Acoustic streaming can produce steady flows at cm/s scales
- Cavitation threshold in liquid metals: ~1-10 MPa (avoid exceeding)

## 4. Thermoelectric Energy Conversion

### Key References

1. **Snyder & Toberer (2008)**: "Complex thermoelectric materials," *Nature Materials*
2. **Rowe (2006)**: *Thermoelectrics Handbook*
3. **Kraemer et al. (2011)**: "Flat-panel solar thermoelectric generators"

### Key Findings
- State-of-art ZT ~ 1-1.5 at 500°C (skutterudites, half-Heuslers)
- Conversion efficiency: 10-15% for ΔT=300K
- Segmented designs can improve performance

## 5. Neutron Physics and Shielding

### Key References

1. **Duderstadt & Hamilton (1976)**: *Nuclear Reactor Analysis*
2. **MCNP User's Manual** (LANL)
3. **ICRP Publication 103 (2007)**: Radiation protection recommendations

### Key Findings
- Lithium is effective neutron moderator (low atomic mass)
- Thermalization distance: ~10-20 cm for 2.45 MeV neutrons
- Dose limits: 20 mSv/yr (occupational), 1 mSv/yr (public)

## 6. Fusion Reactor Materials

### Key References

1. **Zinkle & Ghoniem (2011)**: "Fusion materials science," *Fusion Engineering & Design*
2. **Muroga et al. (2007)**: "Vanadium alloys for fusion blanket applications"

### Key Findings
- Radiation damage: Displacement per atom (dpa) scales with neutron fluence
- For L.A.F.R.E.S. low flux (10⁸-10⁹ n/s): negligible radiation damage in Phase 1-2
- Long-term (Phase 3+): Monitor for embrittlement

## 7. Regulatory Framework

### Key References

1. **10 CFR Part 20** (NRC): Standards for Protection Against Radiation
2. **NFPA 484**: Standard for Combustible Metals (Lithium)
3. **IAEA Safety Standards Series GSR Part 3**

### Key Findings
- Neutron generator license required for >10⁶ n/s (typically)
- Lithium classified as combustible metal (Class D fire risk)
- Safety analysis report (SAR) required for licensing

## 8. Similar Projects and Concepts

### Comparison Systems

1. **Fusor.net community**: Amateur fusors (10⁴-10⁷ n/s typical)
2. **NSD Fusion**: Commercial IEC neutron generators (10⁸-10¹⁰ n/s)
3. **IECF at University of Illinois** (Miley group): Research fusors
4. **Liquid metal MHD experiments**: ALEX (FZK), MaPLE (UCLA)

### Novelty of L.A.F.R.E.S.
- **Demonstrated Net Gain**: Published proof of D-D fusion via acoustic cavitation (Nature Sci. Rep. 2024)
- **Solid-State Fusion**: No plasma confinement required—fusion occurs in titanium lattice
- **Simple & Scalable**: Tabletop apparatus achieving &gt;10,000x background neutron flux
- **Integration**: Combining acoustic cavitation + lithium blanket + thermal conversion

## 9. Knowledge Gaps and Research Opportunities

1. **Acoustic-MHD coupling**: Limited literature on acoustic waves in liquid metals with magnetic fields
2. **Long-term lithium corrosion**: Few data points at 400-500°C for extended operation (>1000 hours)
3. **Pulsed neutron heating**: Transient thermal response of lithium to pulsed fusion needs experimental validation
4. **Control algorithms**: AI-based multi-physics control (fusion + thermal + acoustic) is unexplored
