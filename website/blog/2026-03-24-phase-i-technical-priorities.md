Our near-term work stays focused on **quantitative understanding** of **acoustic amplification of solid-state D–D fusion** in titanium deuteride systems—the line of research described in our [Nature Scientific Reports](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-62055-6) baseline and expanded in the [project plan](/docs/project-plan).

<!-- truncate -->

**Engineering and science tracks (in parallel where possible)**

1. **CFD** — OpenFOAM models of D₂O bubble collapse, validated against experiment  
2. **Microfluidics** — Controlled, monodisperse bubbles and stable emulsions for parametric studies  
3. **Detection** — Multi-channel neutron counting, timing, and background control  
4. **Diagnostics** — High-speed imaging correlated with acoustic drive and neutron signatures  
5. **Scaling** — Documented pathways from demonstrated performance toward application-relevant yields  

**Facility**

Work is centered at **MAXIMUS FUSION SYSTEMS, LLC** in **Naples, Florida** ([lab facilities](/docs/lab/facilities)).

We will post milestone updates here as they mature—always tied to **experimental or modeling results**, not fundraising claims.
