## The Project

We've **demonstrated net-gain nuclear fusion** using acoustic cavitation in titanium deuteride (TiD) systems. Our results are **peer-reviewed and published in Nature Scientific Reports** (May 2024, DOI: 10.1038/s41598-024-62055-6).

**Key Experimental Results:**
- Neutron emission **10,000× above background** (6,000 CPM sustained for hours)
- Acoustic pressure spikes **>24,000 psi** during bubble collapse
- Operating temperature **<400°K** (near-ambient, no plasma confinement)
- Reproducible results with statistical validation

**The Mechanism (Hypothesis):**
Cavitation jets from collapsing D₂O bubbles impact TiD particle surfaces, driving deuterons into collision within the titanium lattice. The solid-state environment may provide:
- High local deuteron density (10²² D/cm³)
- Electron screening (partial shielding of Coulomb barrier)
- Lattice confinement effects

## How You Could Help

We're developing a **DARPA proposal** to quantify and optimize this amplification mechanism. The theoretical physics component needs someone who can:

1. **Calculate electron screening potential** in TiD₂ lattice
   - Use DFT methods to determine deuteron occupancy sites and local electron density
   - Estimate screening potential U_e in the metal environment
   - Compare to known screening factors in other metal deuterides (Pd, Zr, etc.)

2. **Model impact-driven fusion cross-sections**
   - Calculate effective fusion probability under mechanical stress
   - Incorporate lattice confinement and phonon coupling effects
   - Estimate enhancement factor over bare cross-section

3. **Develop scaling predictions**
   - Create quantitative model: Fusion rate = f(impact pressure, deuteron density, material properties)
   - Identify pathways to higher rates (10⁶-10⁹× amplification)
   - Recommend optimal host materials and experimental conditions

**Timeline:** If DARPA proposal is funded (~April 2026), work would be distributed over 18 months with flexible schedule

**Deliverable:** Theoretical analysis and technical report as part of the DARPA program
