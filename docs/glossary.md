# Glossary of Terms

## A

**ALARA** - As Low As Reasonably Achievable. Radiation safety principle requiring dose minimization beyond mere compliance with limits.

**Acoustic Streaming** - Steady fluid flow induced by acoustic waves due to nonlinear effects and momentum transfer.

**Activation** - Process by which stable materials become radioactive through neutron bombardment.

**Alfvén Wave** - Magnetohydrodynamic wave propagating through conducting fluid in magnetic field.

## B

**Blanket** - In fusion context, material surrounding plasma core to absorb neutrons, breed tritium, and/or extract heat.

**Break-even** - Condition where fusion energy output equals energy input (L.A.F.R.E.S. operates far below this).

**Breeding Ratio (TBR)** - Ratio of tritium atoms produced to neutrons consumed; >1 needed for self-sustaining D-T fusion.

## C

**Carnot Efficiency** - Maximum theoretical efficiency of heat engine: η = (T_h - T_c)/T_h.

**CFD** - Computational Fluid Dynamics. Numerical simulation of fluid flow and heat transfer.

**Cross-Section (σ)** - Measure of probability of nuclear reaction; units of area (barns, where 1 barn = 10⁻²⁸ m²).

**Curie (Ci)** - Unit of radioactivity: 1 Ci = 3.7 × 10¹⁰ decays/second (37 GBq).

## D

**D-D Fusion** - Deuterium-deuterium fusion: ²H + ²H → ³He + n (2.45 MeV) or ³H + p (3.02 MeV).

**D-T Fusion** - Deuterium-tritium fusion: ²H + ³H → ⁴He (3.5 MeV) + n (14.1 MeV). Highest cross-section.

**DAQ** - Data Acquisition. System for collecting sensor data.

**Dense Plasma Focus (DPF)** - Pulsed fusion device using pinch effect to achieve high ion density and temperature.

**Dosimeter** - Device measuring absorbed radiation dose (personal or area).

## E

**EAR** - Export Administration Regulations. U.S. controls on dual-use technology exports.

**Electrochemical Potential** - In thermoelectrics, voltage arising from charge carrier concentration gradients.

**Enriched Lithium** - Lithium with altered isotopic ratio, typically >99% ⁷Li (reduces ⁶Li and tritium production).

## F

**Figure of Merit (ZT)** - Thermoelectric material quality: ZT = α²σT/κ. Higher is better.

**FPGA** - Field-Programmable Gate Array. Reconfigurable digital logic device for fast control.

**Fusor** - Common term for Inertial Electrostatic Confinement fusion device.

## G

**Getter** - Material that absorbs impurities (e.g., lithium acts as getter for oxygen, nitrogen).

## H

**Half-Heusler** - Family of thermoelectric materials (intermetallic compounds) with moderate ZT at high temperature.

**Hartmann Number (Ha)** - Dimensionless parameter in MHD: Ha = B L √(σ/μ). Ratio of electromagnetic to viscous forces.

**Heat Pipe** - Passive heat transfer device using phase-change working fluid.

**HMI** - Human-Machine Interface. Operator display and control panel.

## I

**IEC** - Inertial Electrostatic Confinement. Fusion approach using electrostatic fields to accelerate and confine ions.

**ICP-MS** - Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry. Analytical technique for trace element analysis.

**ITER** - International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor. Large tokamak fusion project.

## K

**Knudsen Number (Kn)** - Ratio of molecular mean free path to characteristic length. Kn << 1: continuum flow; Kn >> 1: molecular flow.

## L

**Lawson Criterion** - Condition for fusion break-even: nτT > threshold (3×10²¹ keV·s·m⁻³ for D-T).

**Lithium-7 (⁷Li)** - Stable lithium isotope (92.5% natural abundance). Low neutron capture cross-section.

**Lithium-6 (⁶Li)** - Stable lithium isotope (7.5% natural). High thermal neutron capture: ⁶Li(n,α)³H.

**LLRW** - Low-Level Radioactive Waste. Least hazardous category of radioactive waste.

## M

**MCNP** - Monte Carlo N-Particle. Radiation transport simulation code.

**MHD** - Magnetohydrodynamics. Study of electrically conducting fluids in magnetic fields.

**MPPT** - Maximum Power Point Tracking. Algorithm to optimize power extraction from source (e.g., thermoelectric modules).

**Moderation** - Process of slowing fast neutrons to thermal energies via elastic scattering.

## N

**NRC** - U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission. Federal agency regulating radioactive materials and nuclear facilities.

**Nusselt Number (Nu)** - Dimensionless heat transfer coefficient: Nu = hL/k.

**Neutron Flux (Φ)** - Number of neutrons passing through unit area per unit time (n/cm²/s).

## O

**OSHA** - Occupational Safety and Health Administration. U.S. agency regulating workplace safety.

## P

**PID Control** - Proportional-Integral-Derivative control. Common feedback control algorithm.

**Péclet Number (Pe)** - Ratio of advective to diffusive transport: Pe = vL/α.

**Piezoelectric** - Material generating voltage under mechanical stress (or vice versa). Used in acoustic transducers.

**Plasma** - Ionized gas consisting of ions, electrons, and neutral particles.

**PZT** - Lead Zirconate Titanate. Common piezoelectric ceramic material.

## R

**Radiation Pressure (Acoustic)** - Time-averaged force exerted by acoustic wave on surface.

**Rayleigh Number (Ra)** - Dimensionless parameter for natural convection: Ra = gβΔTL³/(να).

**Rem** - Roentgen Equivalent Man. Unit of radiation dose equivalent (100 rem = 1 Sv).

**Reynolds Number (Re)** - Ratio of inertial to viscous forces: Re = ρvL/μ.

**RSO** - Radiation Safety Officer. Person responsible for radiation safety program.

## S

**SAR** - Safety Analysis Report. Document describing hazards and controls for regulatory licensing.

**SCRAM** - Emergency shutdown. Originally "Safety Control Rod Axe Man" (nuclear reactor term).

**Seebeck Effect** - Generation of voltage across conductor with temperature gradient.

**SIL** - Safety Integrity Level. Classification of safety system reliability (SIL 1-4, higher is safer).

**Sievert (Sv)** - SI unit of radiation dose equivalent. 1 Sv = 100 rem.

**Skutterudite** - Family of thermoelectric materials (e.g., CoSb₃) with good ZT at 300-500°C.

**Sputtering** - Erosion of material surface by ion bombardment.

## T

**Thermalization** - Process by which fast neutrons slow to thermal energies (~0.025 eV).

**Thermoelectric (TE)** - Solid-state energy conversion between heat and electricity.

**TRL** - Technology Readiness Level. Scale from 1 (concept) to 9 (operational system).

**Tritium (³H)** - Radioactive hydrogen isotope. Half-life 12.3 years, β⁻ emitter.

**Tritium Breeding Ratio** - See Breeding Ratio.

## U

**Ullage** - Free space above liquid in container (for thermal expansion).

**UPS** - Uninterruptible Power Supply. Battery backup for critical systems.

## V

**Vacuum Pumping** - Removal of gas from chamber to achieve low pressure (<10⁻³ Torr for fusion).

## W

**Wipe Test** - Method for detecting surface contamination (swipe surface with filter paper, count radioactivity).

## X

**X-ray** - Electromagnetic radiation produced by energetic electron impacts. Hazard in high-voltage devices.

## Z

**ZT** - Dimensionless thermoelectric figure of merit. See Figure of Merit.

## Units and Conversions

### Energy
- 1 eV (electron-volt) = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
- 1 MeV = 10⁶ eV = 1.602 × 10⁻¹³ J

### Radioactivity
- 1 Bq (becquerel) = 1 decay/s
- 1 Ci (curie) = 3.7 × 10¹⁰ Bq = 37 GBq
- 1 mCi = 37 MBq

### Dose
- 1 Sv (sievert) = 1 J/kg (dose equivalent)
- 1 rem = 0.01 Sv = 10 mSv
- 1 Gy (gray) = 1 J/kg (absorbed dose)

### Pressure
- 1 atm = 101,325 Pa ≈ 760 Torr
- 1 Torr ≈ 133 Pa
- 1 bar = 10⁵ Pa

### Temperature
- K (kelvin) = °C + 273.15
- °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

### Cross-Section
- 1 barn = 10⁻²⁴ cm² = 10⁻²⁸ m²
