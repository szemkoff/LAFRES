# Benchmark Devices

## 1. Inertial Electrostatic Confinement (IEC) Fusors

### 1.1 University of Illinois (Miley Group)
- **Neutron Yield**: 10⁸ n/s (D-D)
- **Power**: ~10 kW
- **Key Achievement**: Systematic studies of IEC physics, gridded vs. gridless

### 1.2 NSD Fusion (Commercial)
- **Product**: NSD-Fusion Gradel neutron generator
- **Yield**: 10⁹-10¹⁰ n/s (D-T)
- **Application**: Oil well logging, nuclear security
- **Cost**: $100K-$500K

### 1.3 Amateur Fusors (Fusor.net Community)
- **Typical Yield**: 10⁴-10⁷ n/s
- **Power**: 1-10 kW
- **Key Insight**: IEC feasible with modest budgets ($5K-$50K)

## 2. Liquid Metal Experiments

### 2.1 ALEX (Karlsruhe Institute of Technology)
- **Fluid**: PbLi eutectic
- **Focus**: MHD flow and heat transfer
- **Relevance**: Lithium behavior extrapolatable from PbLi

### 2.2 MaPLE (UCLA)
- **Fluid**: Gallium
- **Focus**: Liquid metal plasma interactions
- **Relevance**: Diagnostics techniques

## 3. Thermoelectric Systems

### 3.1 NASA RTGs (Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators)
- **Materials**: SiGe (older), TAGS/skutterudites (newer)
- **Efficiency**: 6-7% (SiGe), 10-12% (advanced)
- **Lifetime**: 10-20 years (space missions)
- **Relevance**: Long-term reliability benchmarks

### 3.2 Automotive Waste Heat Recovery
- **Companies**: Gentherm, Alphabet Energy (defunct)
- **Efficiency**: 5-8%
- **Challenge**: Cost ($10-$50 per Watt), reliability
