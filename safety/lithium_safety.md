# Lithium Safety Protocols

## 1. Hazards

- **Reactivity**: Violent reaction with water, air (O₂), moisture
- **Fire**: Burns at >1000°C, difficult to extinguish
- **Toxic**: Li₂O fumes (mild irritant, low toxicity compared to other alkali metals)

## 2. Prevention

**Primary**: Sealed system, inert cover gas (argon >99.99%)  
**Secondary**: Double containment, leak detection (O₂ sensors)  
**Tertiary**: Fire detection, Class D extinguisher readily available

## 3. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

- **Handling solid lithium** (<180°C): Safety glasses, nitrile gloves, lab coat
- **Handling molten lithium** (>180°C): Face shield, heat-resistant gloves, fire-resistant clothing

## 4. Fire Response

**DO NOT USE**: Water, CO₂, or standard ABC extinguishers

**APPROVED**: Class D (copper powder, NaCl-based Lith-X™, dry sand)

**Procedure**:
1. Evacuate if fire is large
2. Alert emergency responders (inform of metal fire)
3. Activate argon flood (if available)
4. Apply Class D agent (if safe to approach)
5. Allow to cool before cleanup

## 5. Spill Response

**Small (<1 L, solid)**: Collect with tongs, place in mineral oil

**Large (liquid)**: Evacuate, inert gas blanket, allow to cool/solidify, mechanical removal

## 6. Training Required

All personnel: 2-hour lithium safety course + annual refresher

## 7. References

NFPA 484: Combustible Metals  
MSDS: Lithium metal (Sigma-Aldrich, FMC)
