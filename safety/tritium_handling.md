# Tritium Handling (If D-T Operation)

## 1. Tritium Properties

- **Half-life**: 12.3 years
- **Decay**: β⁻ emission (max energy 18.6 keV, low penetration)
- **Biological hazard**: HTO (tritiated water) readily absorbed, biological half-life ~10 days

## 2. Regulatory Requirements (USA)

**License**: NRC or state, required for >1 Ci (37 GBq)  
**L.A.F.R.E.S. Estimate**: 1-10 Ci inventory (if D-T mode) → requires license

## 3. Containment

**Primary**: Glovebox (negative pressure, HEPA filtered exhaust)  
**Secondary**: Room-level containment, ventilation

## 4. Monitoring

**Air**: Continuous monitoring (ionization chamber or scintillation detector), alarm if >1 DAC  
**Surface**: Weekly wipe tests, liquid scintillation counting  
**Personnel**: Urine bioassay (monthly for workers handling >1 Ci)

## 5. PPE

- Lab coat, double gloves (nitrile)
- Respiratory protection if airborne levels >10% DAC

## 6. Waste

**Contaminated materials**: Sealed in bags, labeled, stored for decay or licensed disposal  
**HTO**: Dilute and discharge (if <regulatory limit) or solidify and dispose as LLRW

## 7. Emergency Response

1. Evacuate contaminated area
2. Isolate ventilation
3. Don PPE (respirator, suit)
4. Contain spill (absorbent, sealed container)
5. Survey and decontaminate
6. Notify regulatory authority if release >reportable quantity

## 8. L.A.F.R.E.S. Baseline

**Phase 1-2**: D-D only (no tritium) → simpler regulatory path  
**Phase 3 (optional)**: D-T mode if higher neutron yield needed
